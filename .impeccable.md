## Design Context

### Users

Sigma Sign is used by people demonstrating, testing, and researching isolated Russian Sign Language recognition from a webcam or uploaded video. The primary job is to understand immediately what the model is seeing, then receive a readable text interpretation without mistaking the beta prototype for a human interpreter.

### Brand Personality

Fast, experimental, and playful. The product should feel technically credible and alive rather than corporate or overly calm. Feedback must stay honest about uncertainty and processing state.

### Aesthetic Direction

Keep the existing dark Sigma Sign palette, with violet as the expressive accent and cyan/green reserved for live and successful states. Use the established liquid-glass visual language selectively around the live recognition surface. Preserve the high-contrast simplified view. Motion should make the transition from recognized gesture tokens to an AI-formatted phrase understandable, not merely decorative.

### Design Principles

1. Show recognition immediately; enhancement may arrive later.
2. Visually distinguish literal model output from AI-formatted language.
3. Make the live state energetic but keep controls calm and readable.
4. Preserve the existing palette and accessibility behavior, including reduced motion.
5. Prefer one clear transformation animation over ambient motion everywhere.
